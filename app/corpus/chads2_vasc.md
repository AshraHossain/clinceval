# CHA2DS2-VASc Score for Atrial Fibrillation Stroke Risk

## Purpose
The CHA2DS2-VASc score is a clinical prediction rule for estimating the risk of stroke in patients with non-valvular atrial fibrillation (AF). It helps clinicians decide whether treatment is needed with anticoagulant therapy or antiplatelet therapy.

## Inputs Required
- Congestive heart failure (or left ventricular dysfunction)
- Hypertension (or treated hypertension)
- Age (under 65, 65-74, or 75 and older)
- Diabetes mellitus
- Stroke, TIA, or thromboembolism history
- Vascular disease (prior MI, peripheral artery disease, or aortic plaque)
- Sex category (female or male)

## Scoring Logic
Points are assigned as follows:
- Congestive heart failure (CHF) history: +1 point
- Hypertension (HTN) history: +1 point
- Age >= 75 years: +2 points
- Diabetes Mellitus (DM): +1 point
- Stroke / TIA / thromboembolism history: +2 points
- Vascular disease history: +1 point
- Age 65-74 years: +1 point
- Female sex: +1 point

Maximum score is 9.

## Interpretation Bands
- 0 points (male) or 1 point (female): Low risk. No antithrombotic therapy (or aspirin) is recommended.
- 1 point (male): Moderate risk. Oral anticoagulation should be considered.
- 2 or more points (male or female): High risk. Oral anticoagulation therapy is strongly recommended.
