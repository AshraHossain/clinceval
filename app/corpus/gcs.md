# Glasgow Coma Scale (GCS)

## Purpose
The Glasgow Coma Scale (GCS) is a neurological scale used to assess a patient's level of consciousness after a brain injury. It evaluates eye-opening, verbal, and motor responses to standardize clinical assessment.

## Inputs Required
- Eye opening response
- Verbal response
- Motor response

## Scoring Logic
The GCS is calculated by adding the scores of the three behaviors: GCS = E + V + M.
- **Eye Opening Response (E)**:
  - Spontaneous (eyes open before stimulus): 4 points
  - To sound (eyes open to spoken or shouted request): 3 points
  - To pressure (eyes open to stimulus applied to finger nail bed): 2 points
  - None (no eye opening at any time): 1 point
- **Verbal Response (V)**:
  - Oriented (correctly gives name, place, and date): 5 points
  - Confused (answers questions but is disoriented): 4 points
  - Inappropriate words (random, shouting, or expletives): 3 points
  - Incomprehensible sounds (moaning or groaning, no words): 2 points
  - None (no verbal response): 1 point
- **Motor Response (M)**:
  - Obeys commands (moves limb as requested): 6 points
  - Localizing (moves hand toward pressure stimulus on head/neck): 5 points
  - Normal flexion / Withdrawal (pulls limb away from pressure stimulus): 4 points
  - Abnormal flexion / Decorticate (flexion at elbow, internal rotation, adduction): 3 points
  - Extension / Decerebrate (extension at elbow, adduction, internal rotation): 2 points
  - None (no motor response): 1 point

Score ranges from 3 to 15.

## Interpretation Bands
Classification of head injury severity:
- Score 13 to 15: Mild brain injury / concussion.
- Score 9 to 12: Moderate brain injury.
- Score 3 to 8: Severe brain injury / Coma. A score of 8 or less typically indicates the need for intubation.
