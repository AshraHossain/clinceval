# HAS-BLED Score for Major Bleeding Risk

## Purpose
The HAS-BLED score estimates the risk of major bleeding in patients with atrial fibrillation who are starting or taking oral anticoagulation. It assists in identifying correctable bleeding risk factors.

## Inputs Required
- Hypertension (systolic BP >= 160 mmHg)
- Abnormal renal or liver function (1 point each)
- Stroke history
- Bleeding history or predisposition
- Labile INRs (high variability or < 60% time in therapeutic range)
- Elderly (age > 65 years)
- Drugs or alcohol use (antiplatelets/NSAIDs, or alcohol excess; 1 point each)

## Scoring Logic
One point is assigned for each criterion met:
- **H**ypertension (Uncontrolled, Systolic BP >= 160 mmHg): +1 point
- **A**bnormal Renal function (dialysis, transplant, or creatinine >= 2.26 mg/dL) OR Abnormal Liver function (cirrhosis, bilirubin > 2x normal, AST/ALT/AP > 3x normal): +1 point each (max 2)
- **S**troke history: +1 point
- **B**leeding history/predisposition (anemia, prior bleed): +1 point
- **L**abile INRs (for patients on warfarin): +1 point
- **E**lderly (Age > 65 years): +1 point
- **D**rugs (antiplatelets, NSAIDs) OR Alcohol (>= 8 drinks/week): +1 point each (max 2)

Score ranges from 0 to 9.

## Interpretation Bands
- Score 0 to 2: Low bleeding risk. Anticoagulation is generally safe.
- Score >= 3: High bleeding risk. Caution and regular clinical review are recommended, and correctable risk factors should be addressed.
