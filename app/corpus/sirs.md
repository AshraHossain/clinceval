# Systemic Inflammatory Response Syndrome (SIRS) Criteria

## Purpose
SIRS criteria are used to identify systemic inflammatory responses to infectious or non-infectious insults. While sensitive, it lacks specificity for sepsis compared to newer models (like qSOFA and SOFA).

## Inputs Required
- Body temperature
- Heart rate
- Respiratory rate (or partial pressure of CO2 in arterial blood)
- White blood cell (WBC) count

## Scoring Logic
One point is assigned for each of the following criteria met:
- Temperature < 36°C (96.8°F) or > 38°C (100.4°F): +1 point
- Heart rate > 90 beats per minute: +1 point
- Respiratory rate > 20 breaths per minute OR PaCO2 < 32 mmHg: +1 point
- WBC count < 4,000/uL, > 12,000/uL, or > 10% immature bands: +1 point

Score ranges from 0 to 4.

## Interpretation Bands
- Score 0 to 1: Negative for SIRS.
- Score 2 to 4: Positive for SIRS. If SIRS is due to a suspected or documented infection, the patient is traditionally diagnosed with sepsis (under the older Sepsis-2 definition).
