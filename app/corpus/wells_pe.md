# Wells' Criteria for Pulmonary Embolism (PE)

## Purpose
Wells' Criteria for Pulmonary Embolism quantifies the pre-test probability of pulmonary embolism (PE) in patients where PE is a diagnostic consideration. It helps determine if further testing (such as a D-dimer test or CT pulmonary angiogram) is required.

## Inputs Required
- Clinical signs and symptoms of deep vein thrombosis (DVT)
- An alternative diagnosis is less likely than PE
- Heart rate (tachycardia)
- Immobilization or surgery in the previous 4 weeks
- Previous DVT or PE
- Hemoptysis
- Malignancy (treatment ongoing, within 6 months, or palliative)

## Scoring Logic
Points are assigned as follows:
- Clinical signs and symptoms of DVT (objective swelling, pain): +3.0 points
- An alternative diagnosis is less likely than PE: +3.0 points
- Heart rate > 100 beats per minute: +1.5 points
- Immobilization (>= 3 days) or surgery in the previous 4 weeks: +1.5 points
- Previous objectively documented DVT or PE: +1.5 points
- Hemoptysis (coughing up blood): +1.0 point
- Malignancy with active treatment within 6 months or palliative: +1.0 point

## Interpretation Bands
Using the Two-Tier Model (Clinical Probability):
- Score 0 to 4: PE Unlikely. Consider D-dimer testing to rule out PE.
- Score > 4: PE Likely. Direct to diagnostic imaging (CTPA).

Using the Three-Tier Model (Clinical Probability):
- Score 0 to 1: Low probability.
- Score 2 to 6: Moderate probability.
- Score > 6: High probability.
